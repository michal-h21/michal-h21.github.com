GIF:

  • Lossless compression
  • Supports 256 colors. Good for pictures that use only a few colors
  • Relies on an algorithm copyrighted by Unisys. However, the patent expired (20 June 2003 in US; a year later in Canada, Germany, France, Italy, Japan, UK).
  • Supports animation
  • The name stands for Graphics Interchange Format
  • Doesn’t support unicode

PNG:

  • Losless compression superior to GIF
  • Recommendation status at W3C: http://www.w3.org/Graphics/PNG/.
  • Developed as a patent-free alternative GIF
  • Doesn’t support animation
  • The name stands for Portable Network Graphics
  • Supports unicode

JPG:

  • Uses lossy compression that discards information the human eye cannot detect.
  • Supports 16 million colors
  • Good for photographs and complex graphics
  • Noticeable reduction in quality for line drawings
  • Named after the committee Joint Photographic Experts Group that defined the standard.
